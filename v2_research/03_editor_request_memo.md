# Memo to the Editor — Building the v2 Casebook
*From the research sweep. What the editor of the case volume needs to decide and provide to move from candidate list to full new version.*

This memo is the handoff. The research is done (~77 verified candidates, eight passes, framework edits proposed). What remains is editorial: decisions only the editor can make, inputs only the editor can supply, and a recommended sequence. Organized as decisions → inputs → sequence → open risks.

---

## A. Decisions the editor must make before drafting

1. **Resolve the missing Case 74.** The v1 PDF build contains 99 cases numbered to 100; Case 74 is absent. Before assigning any v2 numbers, confirm whether Case 74 exists in the Typst sources and was dropped at build, or never existed. This determines the v2 numbering baseline and whether one v1 case needs recovery.

2. **Set the v2 target count.** The sweep produced ~77 verified candidates against a ~108 drafting target, deliberately short on volume in favor of citation rigor. Decide: draft all ~77, draft a curated subset, or commission one more targeted pass (the named thin spots are a physical-domain C5 governance success, more corporate-L&D specifics, and first-person Flywheel accounts). The list is built to support any of these.

3. **Rule on the two evidence-tier cases.** Watson for Oncology (v2-604) rests on journalism-grade sourcing (STAT); the fintech fairness audit (v2-802) is a preprint. Both are teachable; neither meets the peer-reviewed bar the rest of the corpus holds. Decide whether to include with an explicit in-text tier flag, hold pending stronger sourcing, or cut. The colophon's honesty commitments argue for include-with-flag, but this is the editor's call.

4. **Approve the COI disclosure language.** Three cases carry author/institution proximity: CIRCUIT (editor is senior author), BRAIN (research adjacency), Johnson school-surveillance (shared institution). Recommended posture is open disclosure in-text — for CIRCUIT, a visible "an editor is the senior author of the underlying study" note. Approve the exact wording; the book's credibility on these cases depends on it being plain, not buried.

5. **Decide whether the framework edits ship with v2 or precede it.** The competency/CLO revisions (separate document) touch required curriculum. Recommendation: the strongest edits (collaboration-as-unit measurement; human-oversight-of-automated-output; the decision-grade-evidence reframing; the four new CLOs) should be reviewed with the program owner (J. Diamond) *before* case selection hardens, because they change what the cases need to teach. The casebook and the curriculum should not diverge.

6. **Confirm the two-anchor convention.** Each case carries both an induced-competency anchor (1–8) and a LENS mapping (Domain/Problem Type). Decide which is primary in the printed case header. Recommendation: LENS mapping primary (it is the curriculum), induced competency secondary (it is the analytic scaffold). They are close but not identical; the build list records both.

## B. Inputs only the editor/authors can supply

1. **First-person scientist-and-engineer accounts.** The single largest gap the sweep cannot fill by research: the Practice Flywheel and Domain 2 (design iteration) need cases that show *how a capability was built and rebuilt*, which investigation reports structurally cannot provide. These must be authored from practitioner experience, as a distinct evidence class with its own honesty convention (first-person process narration, explicitly flagged, same paraphrasing discipline as the colophon). CIRCUIT and ERKS-class efforts are natural sources. Recommendation: 2–3 such accounts for v2 to establish the class; full coverage is a later edition.

2. **The "unpacking is the method" framing for the front matter.** The recognition that unpacking case studies *is* the LENS method — not a study aid — is the spine of the revision. The editor should write (or commission) the front-matter passage that makes this explicit: each case is a Flywheel turn rendered legible, and the reader learns the discipline by doing it alongside the book. This reframes the whole volume from "catalog of failures" to "method in practice."

3. **Final source re-confirmation for flagged items.** Three sub-claims rest on secondary reporting and must be confirmed against primary sources before print: the FDA pulse-oximetry 2025 draft guidance (v2-209), the BCMA harm-reduction follow-up figure (v2-203), and the pass-1 citations (v2-101–116, verified during pass 1 but not re-pulled this cycle). Primary findings for all are solid; these are belt-and-suspenders checks.

4. **Editorial judgment on the deferred items.** Two items were deliberately deferred to program-owner discussion, not resolved: dual-audience rebalancing (the corpus tilts physical-systems) and the threaded-equity tension (dedicated efforts produced the equity wins, in mild tension with the "thread everywhere" principle). The editor should decide whether v2 names these openly in the front matter or holds them for the curriculum conversation.

3. Confirm the two-anchor header convention (A6) and the target count (A2).

7. Write the "unpacking is the method" front matter (B2).
8. Final source re-confirmation pass on flagged items (B3) before typesetting.

## D. Open risks to track

- **Anchor drift.** The induced competencies and LENS domains are close but not identical. If drafting defaults to the induced anchor and the printed header uses the LENS mapping, cases can end up teaching against the wrong objective. Mitigation: the two-anchor convention (A6) and the build list's dual columns.
- **COI perception.** Three proximate cases are evidentially sound but optically sensitive. Mitigation: plain disclosure (A4); the critical framing on BRAIN; the senior-authorship note on CIRCUIT.
- **Volume pressure.** The ~77/108 gap may tempt padding. The standing brief is explicit: fewer, verified, over more, thin. Mitigation: if more volume is wanted, commission a targeted pass on the named thin spots rather than lowering the citation bar.
- **Curriculum/casebook divergence.** If the framework edits ship in the casebook but not the curriculum (or vice versa), the two artifacts will teach different things. Mitigation: A5 — align with the program owner first.
